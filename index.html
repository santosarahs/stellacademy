Motor Skills
  • Walking and running on different terrains (Grass, sand, paved areas)
  • Climbing (Small climbing structures or natural features like rocks or logs)
  • Throwing and catching (Using lightweight balls or bean bags)
  • Balancing (Walking on low balance beams or along painted lines)
  • Scooting or tricycling (Using age-appropriate ride-on toys)
  • Digging and shoveling (In sandboxes or gardens)
Cognitive Development
  • Nature exploration (Identifying leaves, flowers, insects, or rocks)
  • Simple scavenger hunts (Finding items like "a red leaf" or "a small stick")
  • Sorting and counting outdoor objects (Stones, leaves, or pinecones)
  • Problem-solving (Building towers or obstacle courses with natural materials)
Creative Play
  • Water play (Using buckets, cups, or kiddie pools)
  • Mud painting (Using mud and sticks to create art)
  • Building structures (Using sticks, sand, or blocks to create small houses or castles)
  • Storytelling (Encouraging imaginative role-play outdoors)
Social Skills
  • Group games (Playing cooperative games like "Follow the Leader")
  • Sharing and taking turns (Using swings, slides, or tricycles)
  • Pretend play (Setting up tea parties or playing "camping" with peers)
Sensory Development
  • Texture exploration (Feeling bark, grass, smooth stones, or water)
  • Sound identification (Listening to birds, rustling leaves, or wind)
  • Smelling plants and flowers (Recognizing safe and distinct scents)
Emotional Development
  • Gardening - Planting seeds and caring for plants to foster patience and responsibility.
  • Mindfulness in nature - Sitting quietly to observe the environment and feel connected.
Independence Skills
  • Packing a small bag - Encouraging toddlers to carry essentials for outdoor play.
  • Dressing for the outdoors - Learning to put on jackets, hats, or rain boots.
  • Tidying up - Teaching toddlers to put toys and materials back after playtime.
Educational Activities
  • Color identification - Pointing out colors in nature like leaves, flowers, or stones.
  • Counting steps or objects - While walking or during play activities.
  • Shape recognition - Identifying shapes in leaves, rocks, or clouds.

Sample Resources

Gardening

Introduce your toddler to gardening by helping them plant simple seeds like beans or sunflowers. Use child-safe tools, and let them water the plants daily.

Balancing on Low Beams

Set up a low balance beam or use painted lines on the ground. Encourage your toddler to walk across slowly, holding your hand if needed.

Our Mission

StellAcademy is dedicated to empowering toddlers through the joy of learning and outdoor play. Our mission is to create meaningful experiences that enhance physical, cognitive, and social development in a safe and nurturing environment.

We believe that every child is unique and deserves opportunities to explore, grow, and discover the world around them. By combining structured activities with the magic of outdoor exploration, we aim to ignite curiosity, creativity, and confidence in every child.

"Where every step, smile, and discovery becomes a lifelong memory."
